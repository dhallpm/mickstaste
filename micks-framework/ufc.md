# Micks Picks UFC Framework

## Card Standard
- UFC cards require style matchup, durability, cardio, age/youth gap, and line movement context.
- Do not publish a UFC pick from odds alone.
- Missing tape notes, late replacement context, or weigh-in concerns should downgrade the card to review.

## Core Scoring
- Calculate implied probability from the offered moneyline or prop price.
- Estimate true probability from style, round-winning paths, durability, cardio, finishing threat, and market context.
- EV edge is true probability minus implied probability.
- Best Number and No Bet Cutoff must be strict because UFC prices can move hard after weigh-ins and public content.

## Grading
- A = strong style edge, durable win condition, reliable cardio, current line still playable, and clear source verification.
- B = positive edge with one moderate volatility factor such as round-one danger, takedown dependency, or age curve uncertainty.
- C = small edge or prop-specific angle with limited staking.
- Pass = fragile win condition, bad price, poor durability read, incomplete camp/news data, or public steam without value.

## Style Matchup
- Identify primary win conditions: wrestling control, grappling submission threat, pressure boxing, kicking range, clinch, or durability attrition.
- Compare offensive path to opponent defensive weakness.
- A fighter with one narrow path needs a better price and lower unit size.
- A fighter with multiple repeatable paths can carry higher confidence.

## Cardio And Durability
- Cardio matters most in five-round fights, high-grappling fights, and pressure matchups.
- Durability includes chin, body response, submission defense, damage accumulation, and recovery history.
- Age/youth gap is not automatic. It matters when speed, durability, pace, or wrestling scrambles are central to the fight.

## Market Movement
- Early sharp movement can support a card when it matches the handicap.
- Public-week steam after media picks should be treated as possible inflation.
- Do not chase moved numbers past the no-bet cutoff.

## Safe Version Vs Value Version
- Safe version: moneyline or lower-variance market that aligns with the main win condition.
- Value version: method, round, or plus-money prop only when the finish or decision path is specific and supported.
- If the value version requires too many assumptions, publish the safe version or pass.
